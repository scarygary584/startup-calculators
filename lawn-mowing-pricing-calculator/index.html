Truth-check

Drive time, scheduling gaps, rain, repairs, sharpening blades, small lots, and customer churn can reduce profit. Equipment replacement and vehicle wear are real owner costs, but customers usually will not think about those as separate line items.

Certification / license note

Usually low-barrier, but business registration, insurance, pesticide rules, water rules, and local regulations may matter.

FAQ

Should travel time be counted?

Yes. Route density can make or break lawn mowing profit.

Does this include overhead?

No. Add insurance, marketing, bookkeeping, taxes, repairs, and equipment replacement planning separately.

Can pesticide work require licensing?

Yes. Pesticide and chemical application rules vary by location.
