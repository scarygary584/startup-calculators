What this lawn mowing calculator estimates

The calculator estimates direct cost, target labor pay, profit at the entered job price, a suggested markup price, and profit per on-site hour. It is meant for quick quote math before you say yes to a mowing job.

How to use the numbers

Start with the default $60 visit, then adjust job time, route/fuel cost, and owner pay until the result matches your local market. A small lawn next door to another customer can be profitable at a lower price than the same lawn across town.

Truth-check

Drive time, scheduling gaps, rain, repairs, sharpening blades, small lots, and customer churn can reduce profit. Equipment replacement and vehicle wear are real owner costs, even when customers only see one flat mowing price.

Certification / license note

Basic mowing is often low-barrier, but business registration, insurance, pesticide rules, water rules, and local regulations may matter.

FAQ

Should travel time be counted?

Yes. Route density can make or break lawn mowing profit. A job that looks profitable on-site can become weak if the drive is long.

Does this include overhead?

No. Add insurance, marketing, bookkeeping, taxes, repairs, equipment replacement, and slow-season planning separately.

Can pesticide or weed control work require licensing?

Yes. Pesticide and chemical application rules vary by location. Do not assume mowing rules and chemical application rules are the same.
