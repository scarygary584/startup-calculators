# Startup Calculators Tone Guide

## Core Tone

Use an opportunity-positive tone.

Good opportunity. Real path. Run the numbers first.

These calculators should help entrepreneurs understand the rough math behind a business idea without making the opportunity sound bad, impossible, or foolish. The tone should be practical, respectful, and clear-eyed.

Every calculator should help the user understand the hill, the shovel, and the buckets.

- The hill: the goal, startup cost, revenue target, business challenge, or rule barrier.
- The shovel: the equipment, skill, license path, pricing model, work method, or business setup.
- The buckets: the number of jobs, leads, sales, hours, weeks, months, permits, or steps needed to make the math work.

## Skilled Trades And Regulated Businesses

Do not discourage people from becoming plumbers, electricians, HVAC techs, pest control operators, towing operators, food truck owners, daycare owners, or other serious business owners.

Present skilled trades and permit-based businesses as potentially strong opportunities that may require a real path: training, licensing, permits, insurance, equipment, customer acquisition, pricing discipline, and operating discipline.

It is okay to mention that a well-run trade business can become high-revenue. Always explain that results depend on licensing, pricing, demand, margins, team size, marketing, and operations.

## Plumbing Wording Model

Plumbing can be a high-demand skilled trade and a serious business path. Urgent problems, repeat demand, and skilled work can make it attractive. This calculator is not here to discourage you. It is here to help you understand the rough startup math, training path, licensing costs, insurance, tools, vehicle needs, and customer volume before spending money.

## Avoid

- Do not claim someone will become a millionaire.
- Do not make income guarantees.
- Do not say a business idea will work.
- Do not use hype language.
- Do not frame licensing, permits, insurance, or training as reasons not to pursue a business.

## Prefer

- estimated
- rough planning
- may vary
- before overhead
- check local rules
- real path
- serious business path
- potentially strong opportunity
- results depend on pricing, demand, margins, licensing, marketing, and operations
