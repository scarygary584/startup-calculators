What this means

This calculator estimates monthly revenue, monthly costs, profit before taxes, profit per car, and payback timing for equipment.

Equipment cost can be much lower if you start with a basic setup. Some solo detailers begin with a few hundred dollars in wash supplies, towels, brushes, chemicals, and basic tools, especially if they already have transportation and early customers. A higher equipment cost usually means a more complete mobile setup with a pressure washer, water tank, generator, extractor, polisher, canopy, reels, or a small trailer.

Starter path vs. mobile rig path: Starter path: learn the cleaning process, get a few customers, take before-and-after photos, and reinvest into better tools. Mobile rig path: build a more complete setup for faster jobs, more services, and higher-ticket details.

Truth-check

FAQ

What affects detailing profit most?

Ticket size, speed, route density, supply cost, quality, reviews, and repeat customers matter.

Should mobile detailers check water runoff rules?

Yes. Local water, chemical, and runoff rules may apply depending on where you operate.

Does this include taxes?

No. The profit result is before taxes and before any costs you do not enter.
