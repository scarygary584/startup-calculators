Mobile dog grooming profit examples

Five dogs per day at a $95 average ticket can look strong, but a van payment, fuel, supplies, insurance, maintenance, and cancellations can shrink the route. A tighter route with repeat clients may beat a higher-price day that burns time driving across town.

How to use the numbers

Start with the default average groom price and dogs per day, then adjust van payment, supplies, fuel, and monthly costs. Watch profit per dog and payback timing so the van does not become the boss.

Truth-check

Pet behavior, safety, route density, grooming speed, cancellations, weather, van maintenance, insurance, water/power setup, wastewater rules, and repeat clients can change the math.

Certification / license note

Check local pet grooming rules, business registration, insurance, mobile vendor rules, animal handling requirements, wastewater rules, and local tax requirements.

FAQ

What affects mobile dog grooming startup cost most?

The van or trailer, grooming equipment, insurance, supplies, licensing, and route setup can drive the starting cost.

How do I estimate mobile dog grooming profit?

Estimate average groom price, dogs per day, workdays per month, supplies, fuel, van payment, insurance, maintenance, and other monthly costs. Then compare profit with equipment and van payback.

Should I include van payment?

Yes. A mobile grooming van or trailer can be a major fixed cost and should be included in planning.

Are grooming licenses required?

Rules vary by location. Check business, animal handling, insurance, wastewater, and mobile service requirements.
